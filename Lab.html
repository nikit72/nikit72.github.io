A Hub for the LAB

Challenge
How can we increase publicity of the Lab, UCSD's dance community committee, while helping dancers find dance opportunities?

Tools
Personas, Wireframes, Figma

Role
Lead UX Designer, Researcher, Client Liaison

Solution

A site (mobile & desktop) that consolidates the different dimensions of dance opportunities in beginner friendly terminology while informing dancers of the LAB

Formed 10 years ago, the LAB was created to serve and unify the dance community at University of California, San Diego. While that has stayed as their mission over the years, present day has found the LAB in a precarious situation. While dancers use the LAB’s main communication tool, a Facebook Group, and go to their annual events, the LAB’s lack of organization and brand identity has led to low recruitment rates and a dwindling presence among dancers. My team was assigned to create a site that would counter these issues.

Interviews & Personas

In order to better understand the problem and the needs of our users, we first began by interviewing the LAB staff and people who were interested in dance or already dancers. We discovered there were many different priorities among our interviewees, which allowed us to categorize our main users into these groups: LAB staff, exploring dancer, and dance team leadership.

Using what we learned from the interviews and personas that dove deeper into the different journeys of our users, we then developed key use cases and used card sorting to visualize our users’ mental model of what information would be grouped together.

Ideation

With a sense of how the information architecture of the site should look, we began ideating the structure of the site. As the mobile version would be the most simplified version of the site, we began with sketches of the mobile site. We did a few iterations based on the feedback we received, such as the iteration below.

While people initially expressed interest in a “resources” page, I found that users searched for that information elsewhere. I decided it was more beneficial to create a “get involved” page based on the LAB’s needs, as it primed users for participation before encouraging them to sign up for the LAB. Brand Identity

Once we had a solid wireframe down, the next step was exploring the LAB’s branding. Based on current dance trends and the LAB’s loose theme, we created multiple moodboards to help visualize a new brand identity. Below are moodboards I created, with the first one being chosen for the LAB’s official brand identity.

Discussing the options with our client, we decided dramatic, sleek, dynamic, and science would best represent who they are. This would allow us to highlight dance visuals, creating a sense of awe and emphasizing the most important parts of dance: movement and the people. These beautiful visuals would then pop through bold juxtaposition with dark purple backgrounds. We also wanted to create the distinction that this dance community is at UCSD, a major STEM university, via the science theme.

Prototyping

Once in the prototyping phase, users began giving us more in depth feedback on the content on the site. One of the hurdles we experienced was the text on the site. Due to the name of the organization, a majority of our user testers were confused as to what the Lab was. Through multiple iterations of the homepage, we were able to create a statement that encompasses much of what the Lab represented in a few words.

As we prototyped the mobile site, we began prototyping out the desktop site as well. This allowed us to explore different dimensions and functions only possible on a desktop.

Outcome

The Lab was very satisfied with our final design. The site would be able to help guide new and current dancers in finding where they belong in the dance community, level the playing field for dance teams in exposure, help dance teams communicate with each other, and increase publicity of the Lab. Through our branding, people can see that there are not only dance teams at UCSD, but a thriving dance community. Feel free to explore our mobile and desktop design for the Lab site!

Reflection

As someone who has led a dance team at UCSD myself, this project allowed me to take a step back from my experiences, see the journeys others have taken, and see how much potential the UCSD dance community has. Through the multiple iterations that we went through, I learned the importance of really knowing the industry you are working with in order to tap into that potential. While we could design a site that might look outwardly beautiful, if we do not create a site that will serve the needs of our users, our site will not have truly fulfilled its purpose. While we all had many great ideas for the site, honing in on what would best fit our audience was sometimes very difficult when we had to account for multiple user perspectives. Going through that process shows you all the possibilities rather than failures that you can have until you land on one that fits while accounting for previous scenarios you might not have thought about before. My team especially was great to work with when it came to this. Sharing ideas with each other, being open to new ideas, voicing our opinions and critiques, this was essential in getting us to our final design.
