Sprout your Ideas

Challenge
How can we enhance the creative process of artists to inspire progress in their ideas?

Tools
Storyboards, Paper prototypes, HTML, CSS, JavaScript

Role
Lead UX Designer, Researcher

Solution

A desktop app that organizes a creator's idea process and motivates them to continue their project

Over 30 million people in the US have diabetes, and while it most often develops in adults, the decisions you make and habits you form in the early stages of your life can lead to prevention. Children are not always equipped with the knowledge to make healthy decisions though, especially children in poverty who might not have access to these choices. As members of Global Ties, a humanitarian program focused on solving socially urgent problems, our goal is to introduce this information in a way that is comprehensible, accessible, and fun to children.

Finding those Needs

Art is such a diverse topic! And we wanted to make sure to get those diverse experiences. We began our exploration by observing and interviewing people involved in different art forms in order to view art in their eyes. We went to the source, such as by going out to dance practices or finding people drawing, observing them perform their art related tasks, and asking for interviews. Having both observations and interviews allowed us to see what problems artists were aware of and what problems they might not notice while they create.

The different art forms we focused on was dance, drawing, and comedy. As the activities we targeted varied greatly, there were many interesting notes that came up. One of which that caught our attention across the three art forms was the need to map the creative process and keep track of ideas that spur when you least expect it. .

Storyboards

To get a better sense of how we could target this need, we began to storyboard what a potential user’s creative process might look like. This would give more insight on their behaviors and actions when a problem might occur.

One insight we found really changed how we wanted to target the user’s needs. Many people struggled with a loss of motivation out of frustration in their work or a lack of ideas. While there were moments in their day where they had many ideas and couldn’t sit down to create them, there were other moments where they did have the time but their mood affected their work. Not only did we want our app to help organize the creative process, we wanted to insure that our user would stay inspired and motivated as they created as that had a big impact on what they made.

Paper Prototypes

With our storyboards, interviews, and observations to guide us, the next step was creating paper prototypes of how the user’s interaction with our app might occur. The creative art process is very abstract so how we framed our app would have to fit that unique experience while having a uniform design for multiple users. Below is an example of a prototype I made and user testing we did to get quick feedback.

Wireframes

While ideating many different possible pages our app could have, we came up with several main pages and functions. We then created wireframes of the main features of our app to guide us in our functional prototype.

User Testing

With our prototypes, we then began to do user testing to see if the functions we added were needed in the first place and if the task flows were intuitive. What we found was the appeal of the sprout page and that some functions were more intuitive than others. These notes helped us understand which functions we had to scaffold for the user and how to improve the user’s interactions with the app. Below are some examples of the changes we made.

One change we made was the navbar. We found that it was better to declutter the navbar by turning the "Plant Project" function into a popup rather than add it via the navbar. We also moved the function next to the home button so people would not mistakenly logout.

Another change was scaffolding the "Sprout leaf" function for new users. We found that most users tended to click on the instruction box rather than the button on the navbar. With this information, we decided to turn the description into the button itself. This also encourages people to continue to grow the treeline as the function as now a part of it like a growing tree.

Comparative Evaluations

To ensure that our redesign of the "Sprout Leaf" function was indeed needed, we tested the two versions to see which would be more intuitive for the users. With A/B testing, we found that the redesign decreases the time it take for the new user to click on the function, which was our goal.

Outcome

With a lot of changes made throughout the process, we were able to create a prototype that is now up and running for testing. You can plant a project and sprout with us too!

Reflection

With the topic that my team tackled, it allowed me to explore how the interfaces we use can also contribute the the mood and feelings that we experience while using them. This was especially important with the problem we were providing a solution for as many artists struggled with frustration that hindered their creativity. As the main UX designer, I was learned through many iterations how changing even a single word or location of a function could drastically change how a person. An example of this was the wording I used for a lot of the functions on the app. Instead of timeline, add, or file, words like treeline, sprout, or leaves encouraged the people to interact with the app more and increased their enjoyment with their projects as well. No matter how small the interactions the user might have with your app, you have the chance to make them all count. This project also showed me the importance to not assume what the user will do. A lot of the initial locations of the functions was placed due to our assumption on how users would use the app. Once we began to user test, we found that it was better to design our app to follow their path rather than reformatting our app to make them follow ours. There is a lot of insights you can get from user testing and observations if you are open to listen to them.
